What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners. This policy explains how The Byline uses cookies and similar technologies.

1

Understanding Cookies

Cookies are essential for the modern web experience. They help websites remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and provide personalized content. Without cookies, you would have to log in every time you visit a site or reset your preferences on each visit.

Good to know: Cookies cannot harm your computer or contain viruses. They are simply text files that store small amounts of data.

2

Types of Cookies We Use

We use different types of cookies to provide you with the best possible experience on our website. Here's what each type does:

Essential Cookies

These are necessary for the operation of our website and enable core functionalities such as security, network management, and accessibility. These cannot be disabled.

Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often. This data helps us improve our website's performance.

Functional Cookies

These cookies allow us to remember choices you've made in the past, such as your region, preferred language, or saved preferences.

Targeting Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They may also be used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement.

4

How You Can Control Cookies

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

View what cookies are set
Clear existing cookies
Block third-party or all cookies

Please note: Disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality and experience of our website.

How to manage cookies in common browsers:

Chrome

Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and other site data

Firefox

Preferences > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data

Safari

Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data

Edge

Settings > Cookies and Site Permissions

For more details, visit www.allaboutcookies.org
